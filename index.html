Profile

Translational oncology scientist with over a decade of combined industry and academic experience, driving antibody and small-molecule programs from target validation through IND clearance. Scientific focus across in vivo pharmacology, pharmacodynamic biomarker strategy, mechanism of action characterisation, and preclinical-to-clinical translation for first-in-human filings.

Industry experience spans biomarker strategy and in vivo pharmacology for bispecific antibody programs, immunogenicity and immune-monitoring assay development at a contract research organisation, and IND-enabling pharmacology and toxicology across multiple small-molecule discovery programs. Doctoral research generated the preclinical efficacy and mechanistic data package supporting FDA IND clearance for AG01, a first-in-class anti-progranulin antibody currently in Phase I for advanced solid tumors. Co-inventor on U.S. Patent 9,498,507 B2.

Current research interests sit at the intersection of cancer signalling and chemoresistance: characterising MAP3K8-driven stress-adaptive signalling within the RAS-RAF-MEK-ERK axis as a therapeutic-resistance mechanism in triple-negative breast cancer, evaluating small-molecule and PROTAC degraders of MAP3K8, and integrating R-based bioinformatics with functional in vitro validation to surface clinically actionable resistance nodes and combination strategies.

Featured Projects

TNBC chemoresistance — MAP3K8 × IL-1β joint analysis

R · GEOquery · survival · officer · Claude Code

A self-contained, reproducible R pipeline that grew out of my research at UCSF on MAP3K8-driven chemoresistance, evaluating the joint behaviour of MAP3K8 and IL-1β as distant-relapse biomarkers in chemo-treated triple-negative breast cancer (GSE25066, n=178; independently validated in GSE41998, n=140). Each analysis script ships an auto-generated Methods document with embedded figures, statistics, interpretation and limitations — analysis and publishable methods stay in sync, run after run.

Four-group MAP3K8 × IL-1β median-split DRFS Kaplan-Meier in GSE25066 TNBC (n=178). Doubly-high tumours carry the highest distant-relapse risk; effects are additive (interaction LRT P = 0.86).

R · survival · survminer · GSE25066

Single-gene survival readout for MAP3K8 in the GSE25066 neoadjuvant anthracycline-taxane TNBC cohort (n=178). Median split of probe 205027_s_at, reported as HR for high vs low (low = reference). Used as the prognostic baseline against which the joint MAP3K8 × IL-1β model is built.

IL-1β distant-relapse Kaplan-Meier in chemo-treated TNBC

R · survival · survminer · GSE25066

Companion single-gene readout for IL-1β in the same cohort. Median split on the higher-mean GPL96 probe; reports HR for high vs low. Visualises the late-relapse signal — curves intertwine in the first ~20 months and only separate afterwards, consistent with IL-1β's time-varying hazard (Schoenfeld P = 0.022 in the multivariable Cox model).
